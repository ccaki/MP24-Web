Symptoms

  • Facts
  • Typical rumors & misleading info

Targeted misinformation

How to check it

News that is misleading, which is produced to deceive and do harm which can help them achieve the intended purposes. For example:

1.5G WiFi networks could be responsible for the rapid spread of the new coronavirus.

2.One or some of the Asian countries has produced the Covid-19 (increase the race conflict)

Sloppy reporting

How to check it

News that contains some grains of truth that are not fully verified, which are used to support a certain position or view. For example:

1. Achieve herd immunity by “natural” infections.

2. Masks were not recommended for the general public in the early stage of Covid-19 pandemic

Misleading news

How to check it

Misleading news that’s not based on facts, but supports an on-going narrative – news where there is no established baseline for truth, often where ideologies or opinions clash and unconscious biases come into play. For example:

1. Injecting bleach or highly concentrated rubbing alcohol can help combat the virus

2. The children are virtually immune to Covid-19.

3. Covid-19 will disappear like a miracle in summer.
