How to distinguish real news and fake news for Covid-19?

Consider the source Check the reliability of the source. Some of the source may reference “Harvard University” or ”Japanese doctors”. Check on the official site if the expression is repeated there. If not, the story is probably a rumor. Logo Check if the articles or news use the same logo as the official one. Bad English Expression Credible journalist or organization are less likely to make spelling, grammar mistakes or write entirely using capital letters. If the above things happen, you should cast serious doubt on the reliability of the news. Mimic the official site Some fake accounts mimic the official site. For example, they may have a twitter account called @ABCNewsTonight which may try to mimic the official @ABCNews account. By doing so, they produce some news to deceive and do harm which can help them achieve the intended purposes. Over-encouragement to share If a message push you to share with your friends or families. Please be care of. That’s may be start of the viral transmission. Use fact-checking website If you have any doubt about the reliability of the news, please use the useful fact-checking website such as APFactCheck and Full Fact to check the reliability of the news
