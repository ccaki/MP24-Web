Common misleading information type

News that is misleading, which is produced to deceive and do harm which can help them achieve the intended purposes.

News that contains some grains of truth that are not fully verified, which are used to support a certain position or view.

Misleading news that’s not based on facts, but supports an on-going narrative – news where there is no established baseline for truth, often where ideologies or opinions clash and unconscious biases come into play.

Misleading information samples

#1: The vaccine will give me COVID-19.

see more on Cleveland Clinic

Vaccines prime your immune system to recognize and fight off a disease, but they don’t actually cause an infection. You might have some fatigue, muscle aches, a headache or a fever after you get the vaccine. That’s normal with any vaccine – it’s a sign that your immune system is responding.

#2: The vaccines are not safe for people with allergies.

see more on MediResource Inc.

If you have a history of severe allergic reactions to any component of the COVID-19 vaccine, you should not receive it. Talk to your health care professional if you think you may be allergic to one or more of the vaccine ingredients.

#3: We don’t know what’s in these vaccines.

see more on Cleveland Clinic

Both Pfizer and Moderna have published the ingredient lists for their vaccines. In addition to the star ingredient, the COVID-19 mRNA for the spike protein, both vaccines contain lipids (fats) that help deliver the mRNA into your cells and a few other common ingredients that help maintain the pH and stability of the vaccine. Despite theories circulated on social media, they do not contain microchips or any form of tracking device.

#4: The vaccine was developed to monitor people.

see more on MediResource Inc.

The COVID-19 vaccine was developed to prevent people from getting COVID-19. There is no evidence that the vaccine has microchips in it that would track you or your personal information.

#5: The mRNA vaccine will alter my DNA.

see more on MediResource Inc.

The Pfizer-BioNTech and Moderna COVID-19 vaccines contain mRNA, which are genetic instructions on how to make proteins. After getting the vaccine, your cells use the mRNA to make the coronavirus spike protein, then break down and destroy the mRNA after it's been used. The mRNA from the COVID-19 vaccine never enters the nucleus of the cell, which is where our DNA is stored. This means that the mRNA does not affect or interact with our DNA at all.

#6: The vaccine will cause infertility.

see more on MediResource Inc.

Because the COVID-19 vaccines do not contain the live virus (remember, it’s an mRNA vaccine), they are not thought to cause increased risk of infertility, first or second trimester loss, still birth or congenital anomalies. Additionally, there is no evidence to suggest that the vaccine is a risk to a breastfeeding baby.

How to distinguish real news and fake news for Covid-19?

  • 1. Consider the source

    Check the reliability of the source. Some of the source may reference “Harvard University” or ”Japanese doctors”. Check on the official site if the expression is repeated there. If not, the story is probably a rumor.

  • 2. Check the logo

    Check if the articles or news use the same logo as the official one.

  • 3. Bad English Expression

    Credible journalist or organization are less likely to make spelling, grammar mistakes or write entirely using capital letters. If the above things happen, you should cast serious doubt on the reliability of the news.

  • 4. Mimic the official site

    Some fake accounts mimic the official site. For example, they may have a twitter account called @ABCNewsTonight which may try to mimic the official @ABCNews account. By doing so, they produce some news to deceive and do harm which can help them achieve the intended purposes.

  • 5. Over-encouragement to share

    If a message push you to share with your friends or families. Please be care of. That’s may be start of the viral transmission.

  • 6. Use fact-checking website

    If you have any doubt about the reliability of the news, please use the useful fact-checking website such as APFactCheck and Full Fact to check the reliability of the news
