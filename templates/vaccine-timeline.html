The novel Corona Virus was declared as a global pandemic depicting significant effect on the lives of millions. Lockdowns were imposed in multiple cities worldwide leading scientists and health care officials to go scourging for a vaccine to a health scare like never seen before in recent times.

February 2020

Gaining unprecedented traction, the “Operation Warp Speed (OWS)” was introduced by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services in a bid to acquire a Covid-19 vaccine.

Keeping all safety measures in check, two companies namely Pfizer and Moderna undertook a prolonged vaccine technology project known as Messenger RNA (mRNA) vaccines.

March 30, 2020

Carving a niche as leaders of Covid-19 vaccine technology, Moderna published Phase l/ll trial data on July 14, 2020 whereas Pfizer released the data on August 12, 2020.

Both vaccines were deemed effective when tested on vaccinated animals showing prevention of viral replication in the nose resulting in the inception of Phase lll trials.

July 2020 – August 2020

As enrolment for Phase lll trials were rolled out, almost 70,000 people registered to get vaccinated with Moderna leading vaccine testing that was US-centred whereas Pfizer decided to drive its focus on a global magnitude.

The status of Covid-19 in the tested people was tracked for 10 weeks as they went about their normal lives during this period.

September 2020

Showing promising results, Pfizer’s Phase lll data displayed an efficacy rate of 95%. Similarly, Moderna also displayed an efficacy rate of approx. 95% with traces of any adverse effects being only observed in the placebo arm and not the vaccine arm.

The positive bearings of the results were presented to the U.S. FDA and official statements were released by both parties that Moderna hoped to roll out 20 million doses and Pfizer aimed for 50 million vaccine doses to be globally existing by end of 2020.

November 2020

UK distributed the controversial AstraZeneca vaccines and yet failed to control the number of cases deciding to mix doses of AstraZeneca and Pfizer. Health officials and pharmacies had started giving out vaccines with the highest priority given to patients with high heart failure conditions.

Most countries have suspended AstraZeneca vaccine however positive results have been shown in the patients who were vaccinated with both Moderna and Pfizer doses in the Phase l/ll trial indicating the rollout of the 2 vaccines will continue. WHO aspires to get global vaccination by the end of 2022.
